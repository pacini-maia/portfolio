About Me

I’m a technical communication graduate student interested in content analysis, corpus linguistics, and TPC pedagogy. My work focuses on how research methods are learned, applied, and communicated in scholarship and in practice.

When first learning content analysis, I ended up creating a “codebook” to make my own coding more consistent before knowing what a codebook even was. When looking for examples to refine it, I struggled to find clear methods and examples in published work. Given everything I learned about coding afterwards, it stuck out to me that there wasn’t a clear way to learn about this method that ultimately aims for increased clarity and transparency.

Since then, my work has focused on developing tools and resources that help TPC scholars gain exposure to quantitative and mixed-methods research practices. I’ve researched methods reporting practices across articles in the field to identify gaps and inconsistencies in how technical communication scholars discuss the agreement process. I’ve also used my knowledge of content analysis to create supplementary tutorials and other course aids for students learning content analysis.
